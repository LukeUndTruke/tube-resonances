Acoustic Tube Resonances

This interactive simulation visualizes one-dimensional acoustic waves in cylindrical and conical ducts, allowing direct comparison of two different geometries, especially cylinders and cones. The plots show the spatial pressure and volume velocity fields together with the corresponding input impedance spectrum.

The geometry can be changed independently for the left and right systems. Cylinders are defined by length L and radius R, while cones are defined by length L (the slant length in this case) and end radii R1 (left) and R2 (right). The complex termination impedance with real part Zr and imaginary part Zi controls the acoustic boundary condition at the end of the tube. The boundary condition at the input end is chosen to be a harmonic volume velocity with fixed unit amplitude. Speed of sound and density inside the geometry are those of air.

The frequency slider selects the excitation frequency shown in the field plots and impedance spectra. Additional controls allow adjustment of the visual scale, animation speed, particle density, and particle displacement exaggeration.

Note that the sliders for the impedance as well as those for the particle displacement exaggeration are logarithmic.
